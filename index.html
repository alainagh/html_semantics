What Are They?

Bonobos are an endangered great ape. Although originally mistaken for small chimpanzees, these apes are actually a distinct species.

More Information

Diet
More than half of a bonobo's diet is made up of fruit and leaves. However, they also eat things besides plant matter such as honey and meat from small animals.
Habitat
Bonobos live in humid forests. They are found south of the Congo River.
Characteristics
These apes are sensitive and compassionate creatures. However, they get more cross with age. They are social, sexual creatures. Instead of males leaving their groups as in the case of wolf packs and lion prides, bonobo females will leave instead. This is what mixes the ape gene pools.
